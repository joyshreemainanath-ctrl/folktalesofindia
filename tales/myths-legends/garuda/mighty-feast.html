Garuda's Mighty Feast

Reading time: 4 to 5 min

Cycle: Garuda (The King of Birds)Source: Tale of Astika - Adi Parva, MahabharataTheme: Hunger, Restraint, Wisdom

Satiating an unimaginable hunger while navigating the safety of ancient, hanging sages.

Garuda's hunger was so immense that ordinary prey could not satisfy him. Yet each act of feeding became a test of judgment, not just strength.

Among his greatest trials was preserving the lives of sages while seeking sustenance, proving that force without discernment is incomplete.

The strong may seize, but the wise preserve;
true power protects what it could destroy.

This chapter prepares Garuda for his assault on heaven and the impossible task ahead.
