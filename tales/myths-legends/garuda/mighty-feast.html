Garuda's Mighty Feast

Reading time: 4 to 5 min

Cycle: Garuda (The King of Birds)Source: Tale of Astika - Adi Parva, MahabharataTheme: Hunger, Restraint, Wisdom

Satiating an unimaginable hunger while navigating the safety of ancient, hanging sages.

Kashyapa directed Garuda to a lake where the elephant and tortoise clashed fiercely, urging him to consume them and gain the strength needed for his quest. Without hesitation, Garuda descended with immense force, seizing the elephant in one claw and the tortoise in the other before soaring into the sky. As he flew, the wind from his wings caused divine trees to tremble, their golden branches quivering in fear of his immense power.

Spotting a massive banyan tree with a sprawling branch extending a hundred leagues, Garuda attempted to perch, but his sheer weight caused the branch to snap. As he looked down, he noticed the Valakhilya sages hanging upside down from the broken branch. To avoid harming them, he carefully grasped the branch in his beak and searched for a safe place to release it.

Flying over vast lands and mountains, Garuda struggled to find a location. He approached his father, Kasyapa, who guided him to a remote, snow-covered mountain uninhabited by men. Upon reaching the secluded peak, Garuda finally let go of the massive branch, which crashed down, shattering the mountain peaks, hitting the trees and sending blossoms into the air.

With his burden released, Garuda settled atop the mountain and feasted on the elephant and tortoise, fully satiating his hunger and preparing himself for the great task of retrieving the elixir.
